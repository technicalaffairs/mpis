Introduction

This procedure details the major maintenance procedure P1 for the circuit breaker, which should be carried out five years or after 2000 operation, as indicated by Y5 in the above title block. The first draft document initiated by Cairo zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated completely and properly earthed from both sides,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Isolate the DC voltage for the earthing switch,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • DC control power should be disconnected.

  • Before using a blower to clean the control cabinet or when cleaning the insulators, wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks.

  • Hydraulic test gauges, hydraulic oil, and SF6 gas if needed,

  • SF6 test gauge, micro ohmmeter, and Air Blower,

  • Dew point equipment, acidity equipment, Air content equipment, and timing equipment

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the high voltage terminals.

  2. Visual check and cleaning the insulators.

  3. Measure the humidity of the SF6 gas (Dew point).

  4. Check the tightness of the compressed air pipe work and couplings.

  5. Check the terminals in the control cubical.

  6. Visual Check of the control cubicle.

  7. Check holding down bolts of the base.

  8. Check the paint finish.
