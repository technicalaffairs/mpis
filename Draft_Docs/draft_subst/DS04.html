Introduction

This document lists the Minor Maintenance Procedures P1, which must be performed every one year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions
  1. A work permit must be issued.
  2. The Isolator must be Isolated from two sides.
  3. Local grounding must be at the two side of the IS. SW.
  4. Use only fiberglass ladder.
  5. Switch off the control circuit (DC Voltage

Tools and Equipment
  1. One set of open-end wrenches.
  2. Pure bingeing grease and wipers.
  3. Micro ohm tester.

Work to be Carried Out

 1

Inspect the insulators, if porcelain shows shops and scratches , replace them by new one using a special device for this purpose see Fig 1. 
Note:

  • At replacement check the vertical position of the columns carefully check the bolted

  • connections of supporting columns and the turning columns in particular for the

  • Proper tightening.

 2

Clean the surface of the insulators from dust, dirt and other deposits for cleaning purposes use benzene .

If the contact surface shows burning traces clean these spots or replace the damaged parts.

3

Check the contact pressure and adjust it.
Note:

  • Use the same thickness of the reference copper plate is equal to that of the contact blade the pulling out effort should be 10 Kg force for each pair of the lamels

  • If the pressure is less tighten the bolts and nuts of the split contact of current carrying conductors and parts.

  • After checking the split contact clean the flexible coupling [ 1 Fig 2 ] , spring [ 2 Fig2] , lamel [ 3 Fig 2 ] and blades [ 4 Fig 2] with pure benzene and grease ity

4

Check all gears for the proper operation and see that there is no jamming or wearing of the parts and replace certain parts if necessary.

5

Check the circuitry of the operation mechanism. Clean all parts and grease all friction parts of the gears and drivers.

 6

Check the grounding of the circuit breaker for the proper state.

 7

Check the value of the main blade current circuit resistance (micro ohm Test) (it should be within 10 ohm). If the circuit resistance exceeds the indicated value reduce it to the rated value by adjusting the contact pressure and by tightening the bolted connections.
