Introduction

This document details the overhaul maintenance procedure P3, which must be performed every 3000:4000 cycles, mechanical operations as indicated at the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Khaled Ragaa Zaky at Taba 500, Canal zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Ensure that the CB isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of CB is required.

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,..., etc".

  • TM1600 tester, dew point tester, DC contact resistance tester,

  • Complete set of tools, torque wrenches, MOLYKOTE M111 grease (-01835265)

  • Screws driver diameter 4 mm for terminals of the "Entrelec" type (for example)

  • RUMCOAT EEVA primer by DERIVERY ref 333103

  • Polyurethane lacquer 780 by DERIVERY, ref. to match the colour of equipment.

  • A400 emery paper, brush, solvent,

Work to be carried out 

 

General Tightness of Hydraulic Circuit

  1. Check that there are no remnants of oil (seeping) under hydraulic connections. Should this be necessary, re-tighten screws on the connections concerned to the recommended torque are given in paragraph of screw tightening torques   (figure 6).

    CAUTION: This type of intervention should always be performed with the circuit-breaker de-pressurized. before acting, bring the hydraulic circuit pressure to zero procedure described in section "pre-commissioning inspections and testes"

  2. Check for substantial leak (drip after drip) if detected during a maintenance visit, a repair operation should be programmed as quickly as possible in accordance with the lock-out possibilities of the apparatus. The following operations must be performed with the apparatus out of service, mechanically and electrically isolated and earthed:

  3. Bring the hydraulic circuit to zero pressure,

  4. Remove the faulty union,

  5. Clean the parts,

  6. Re-install union with a new seal

  7. Check for internal leak due to a number of pump starts above the permissible average (4 per 24 hours). Do not count starts following circuit-operations. Quickly get in touch with GEC ALS-THOM T&D S.A. Aftersales Service

Receiver Oil Level

  1. Check that the receiver oil level should always be between the limits given by the column level indicator. If oil lacks, top up using hydraulic oil complying with regulations AIR 3520/B or USA-MIL 05606.

CAUTION:

If the level observed very low (below limits) check tightness of all hydraulic circuits to detect eventual leaks and make inspections more frequently, until the leak is found and repaired.

Operation Counter

  1. Read indications given by the counter(s) and note these on the inspection card "maintenance visits". The number of circuit-breaker operations recorded influences future maintenance operations.

Pump-start Counter

  1. Read pump-start counter indications and note these on the service card "maintenance visits"

  2. If the average number of pump-starts between 2 maintenance visits is higher than 4 per 24 hours, do not count starting after circuit breaker operations- with no other signal but "pressure decrease", an internal leak exists in the hydraulic system. Quickly get in touch with GEC ALS-THOM T&D S.A. After sales Service

Tightening of mountings

  1. Check fixations tightening of elements not pressurized (frame, cubicle, ...),

  2. Check and adjust, with a torque wrench, the tightening torques of screws on sub-assemblies not subjected to oil or gas pressure. The tightening torques are given in paragraph of screw tightening torques (figure 6).

Accumulator Pre inflation Pressure

  1. Check the pre-inflation pressure of accumulators

  2. If an accumulator does not show the correct pre-inflation pressure, replace it

Oil Pressure Switch Thresholds

  1. Check the switching thresholds of each oil pressure switch contact,

  2. If values are outside required tolerances replace the oil pressure switch,

Relay Operation

  1. Check relay operation by executing the following circuit-breaker sequences:

    Closing lock-out, Automatic opening, Anti-pumping,

  2. Check that the following signalizations are working: Oil pressure decrease, SF6 pressure decrease

Tightening of LV Terminals

  1. With the electric cubicle out of service, check that the clips and tips of conductors are tightened correctly and check the torque of connection mountings.

Tools to be used : screws driver diameter 4 mm for terminals of the "Entrelec" type (for example)

CAUTION : screws on terminals must not be over-tightened.

 

Oil Filter         See figure 1

CAUTION:

The following operations should be performed with electrical circuits de-energized and hydraulic circuits de-pressurized.

Change the oil filter (72.40) in the low pressure receiver of the re-inflation unit (70.00), by following the procedure below:

  1. Remove the re-inflation until (70.00) cover (73.00)

  2. Disconnect the electric feeding connector from the re-inflation unit

  3. Uncouple HP and LP pipes at their arrival on the re-inflation unit

  4. Remove mounting screws of the hydraulic component supporting plate on the re-inflation unit

  5. Put the lifting rings in place

  6. Slowly lift the hydraulic component supporting plate

CAUTION: Part of the apparatus is immersed in oil, lifting should be slow to avoid oil splashing

  1. Wait for a few minutes, allowing the oil to drain off,

  2. Remove the filter cartridge mounting nut (72.41).

  3. Remove the filter cartridge (72.40) and seal.

  4. Install a new filter cartridge and seal.

  5. Re-tighten using the nut.

  6. Re-install and attach supporting plate, piping connectors and cover.

  7. Connectors and cover. Before resumption of service bleed hydraulic circuits,

Figure 1:  Oil filter

Insulating Envelopes

  1. Check the condition of insulating envelopes, these should be free from impact marks, splintering cracks, dust deposits, pollution etc ...

  2. If this the case, clean with a dry rag.

  3. If anomalies are found contact : GEC ALSTHOM T&D S.A. After sales Service in order to change the defective parts

Operating Times of Poles and Auxiliary Contacts

  1. Measure the operating times of poles and auxiliary contacts at rated voltage and pressure.

  2. If the values found are outside the acceptable contact GEC ALSTHOM T&D S.A. After sales Service.

Measurement of Contact Resistance

  1. Measure the main circuit's resistance

  2. If the values found are outside the acceptable, contact GEC ALSTHOM T&D S.A. After sales Service.

Hydraulic Control Mechanism

  1. Check condition of the hydraulic control mechanism: re-inflation unit, ram, operational block, accumulators,

CAUTION:

This operation necessitates complete dismantling of hydraulic control mechanism sub-assemblies to gain access to the elements which are to be inspected, and should obligatorily be performed by:

GEC ALSTHOM T& D S.A. After sales Service, to be contacted before all operations of this kind, preferably at the maintenance planning stage

Interruption Chambers

  1. Check the condition of interruption chambers,

  2. This operation necessitates complete dismantling of the interruption chamber to gain access to elements which to be inspected and should obligatorily be performed by GEC ALSTHOM T& D S.A. Aftersales Service, to be contacted before all operations of this kind, preferably at the maintenance planning stage.

Lubrication of Operating Rod         (grease-box) Figure 2

  1. Fill the grease boxes at the foot of each column with SI3 grease

  2. Remove cover (22.10)

  3. Introduce grease "PG 54" through the grease nipple (22.13)

  4. Put back the cover (22.10), its seal (22.11) and screws (22.12)

Figure 2: Lubrication of operating rods
