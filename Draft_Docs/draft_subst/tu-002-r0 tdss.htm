Introduction

The E1610 is a hand held digital Earth Tester.

The E1610 uses the four terminal measurement method on which the resistance of the current circuit test leads 
does not affect the reading

The E1610 can also be used for the three terminal measurement methods with reduced accuracy.

Measures: 1. Earth resistance

2. Earth continuity

3. Soil resistively

Batteries: six 1.5 volt AA

Information

Accessories

One 50 meter test lead

One 30 meter test lead

Two 5 meter test lead

Four straight test spikes

One mallet

Resistance 0 –2000 Ω

Earth resistance in the range of 0 – 2000 Ω can be measured and the value displayed on the LCD.

Battery Low

If the battery voltage is too low the “ Bat low “ symbol will appear on the LCD. Turn tester to 20 scale to check battery condition.

Reverse polarity

If the potential leads are reversed with respect to the current leads a “MINUS “ sign will be displayed on the LCD. The reading is still valid but the potential leads should be reversed to remove the “ MINUS" sign.

High Current Spike Resistance

If the Rc LED lights the test spike resistance of the current circuit is too high. This will prevent the operator taking a valid reading if the RC sign is not corrected .the circuit is activated when the tester is switched on and will remain during the test 
if not corrected.

An Rc fault may be caused by an open circuit or poor connection in the test lead, high resistance in the ground in the vicinity 
of the current test spike or poor contact with the spike.

High Potential Spike Resistance

When the resistance of the potential circuit is too high the LED marked RP will illuminate. This test is not automatic, 
but should be as a preliminary test carried out by the operator, by pressing the button marked “ TEST RP”. An RP fault 
may be caused by an open circuit or poor connection to the test lead, high resistance in the ground in the vicinity of the potential spike or poor contact with the spike.

Operating the “ Test RP" button will cause the LCD to read over- range during this test.

Interference Noise

The LED marked “ Noise” will automatically illuminate when the interference voltage in the earth being measured exceeds the level, which can be rejected by the tester. A valid measurement cannot be made in this condition. The solution may be to wait until the interference, if temporary subsides or to change the position of the test spike.

Directives

Basic Test Procedure

After the test spikes have been set up and connected to the tester:

  1. select the measuring range required
  2. check that no adverse test conditions are displayed for example high current circuit resistance, noise interference or battery low are not displayed
  3. Press the “ Test Rp" button to check potential spike resistance. The RP LED should not be illuminated.
  4. If all conditions for the test are satisfactory then the reading on the display may be accepted as the earth resistance.

 

 

 

 

 

 








 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Measuring the Resistance of earth Electrodes

With the fall of Potential Method

This test requires two spikes

Connect the earth tester C1 and P 1 to the earth electrode to obtain reliable results the distance between the electrode under test and the current spike should be at least ten times the maximum dimension of the electrode system

For example 30 meters for a 3 meter electrode

Three readings are taken
  1. With the potential spike midway between the electrode and the current spike
  2. At a position 10% of the electrode to current spike distance back towards the electrode
  3. At a position 10% of the distance towards the electrode Calculate the average of the three readings find the average maximum deviation of the readings from this average in ohms Express this deviation as a percentage The accuracy of this measurement using this method is 1.2 times the percentage deviation of the readings A measurement accuracy of 2% is obtainable

A reading that differs more than 5% is unacceptable and the reading should be taken again with a larger separation between the electrode and the current spikes

The 62% rule Measuring Method

Used when the readings using the fall of potential method disagree beyond the required accuracy

This rule states: The true resistance of the earth electrode is equal to the measured resistance when the potential spike is positioned 62% of the distance between the earth electrode and the current spike, away from the earth electrode.

This rule only applies:

  1. When the earth electrode and both the current and potential spikes are in a straight line the soil is homogeneous
  2. The earth electrode has a small resistance area this method can only be used on small earth systems consisting of single rod or plate and medium systems using several rods.

A set of readings taken with different test spike spacing gives a set of resistivities which, when plotted against spacing , indicates whether there are distinct layers of soil or rock and gives an idea of their respective resistivities and depth.

Safety precautions

  1. When placing spikes in the ground make sure there are not buried cables below the surface.
  2. Do not turn on instrument before people are away from the test area
  3. Switch off instrument before disconnecting any leads in the test circuit
  4. Use caution when working in an electrical environment and be aware of live apparatus.
