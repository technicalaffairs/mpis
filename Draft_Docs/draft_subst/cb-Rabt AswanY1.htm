Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases or every 200 compressor operating hours, whichever occurs first as indicated  by Y1 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated at Rabt Aswan 220kv substation upper Egypt zone ECET.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Appropriate personal protective equipment must be used, safety shoes, helmet breathing mask, safety glasses
  • The circuit breaker should be switched off. and fully isolated.
  • The earthing switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker
  • Switch off control current for CB (d.c)
  • Switch on the ventilation.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety gloves, breathing, helmet, shoes
  • Complete set of tools, rags, Blower
  • Portable pressure gauges.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Inspect and clean out the compressor air filter.

  2. Drain and refill compressor sump with air compressor lubricant.

  3. Check the compressor safety valve for correct operation manually lifting the valve plungre and lefting the plunger close by itself

  4. Check the pressure drop caused by the safety valve operation and by the subsequent air pressure recovery as follows:
        * The operating points of all pressure-operated contacts 63,63L,63AL, and 63R
        * The pressure at which the safety valve recloses
        * The compressor pump-up time

  5. Make sure that all contact circuit terminals and auxiliary switch contacts are clean and tightened,

  6. With control circuits de-energized, make sure that all pivot pins of the bistable spring devise and the auxiliary contact links of each pole are clane and lubricated If necessary, clean out, inspect and re-lubricate the pins, using MP MOLYSLIP grease.

  7. With control and indicator circuits energized, check the operating pressure of the gas alarm and interlock contacts 63GAL and 63G of the gas pressure gauge, by loosening and refightening the pressure gauge coupling so as to produce a transient, iocal pressure drop sufficient to close and re-open the gauge contacts and whether the operating pressures comply with the assigned values.

  8. If nessary, refill each breaker pole with SF6 gas

  9. Useing soapsuds, make sure that the gauge couplings are gas-tight again
