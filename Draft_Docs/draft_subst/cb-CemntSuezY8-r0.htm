Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedure P1, to be performed every a maximum of eight years as indicated by Y8 in the title block or after 1500 operations.

The first draft initiated by eng Ibrahem Ibrahem Shehata at Sues Cement substation 220kv Canal zone

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes safety harness

  • Circuit breaker isolation and earthing is required.

    •

  • Before starting maintenance or inspection jobs requiring the enclosure to be opened remember to isolate and earth the switchgear assembly on which work is to be carried out, even prior to drawing off the SF6 and opening the enclosure. Release the hydraulic pressure and disconnect the auxiliary supply. Adhere to the safety regulations.

Environmental Precautions

  • Before using a blower for cleaning from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, and Gas detector

Work to be Carried Out

General Precautions

  1. The spring washers, split pins and tap washers of any screwed joint opened for maintenance purposes must be replaced.

  2. The same applies to all seals and gaskets exposed during external metal parts should be lubricated with Tecty1 506 at intervals of 2 to 3 years.

  3. All open breaker parts should be covered to protect them against the ingress of dirt while assembly is in progress. Any switching dust which has settled in the breaker and on the built-in parts during operation should be removed immediately the breaker is opened and any subassemblies have been removed. When this dust is exposed to the air for some time it will absorb moisture and can then only be removed with difficulty.

  4. Gloves should be worn.

  5. Wipe off the dust with a rag or use a vacuum cleaner.

  6. Do not disturb the dust unnecessarily

  7. Throw the dust and the rag into a refuse bin as soon as the job is finished.

Visual Check

  1. Check SF6 pressure and, if the values do not tally, test the pressure gauges and check the breaker for leaks.

  2. Read and record the counter reading (where applicable).

  3. Examine paint finish and touch up damaged sections.

  4. Draw off the SF6

Interrupter unit

  1. Open one interrupter unit of each breaker pole, check the contacts and replace them if they show signs of severe are erosion If contact replacement proves to be necessary, see mpis docs CB-148-r0

  2. Replace filters.

Pole Column

  1. Auxiliary switches sparingly oil the contacts and bearings, examine and grease the coupling linkage.

  2. Fill in the SF6

Electro Hydraulic Operating Mechanism

  1. Check the oil level.

  2. Examine all pipe joints for correct sealing.

  3. Drain off the oil and clean both the oil tank and the intake filter.

  4. Fill in the oil.

  5. Measure the provisional nitrogen pressure on the oil side.

  6. Raise the oil pressure in the storage sy1inder to the nominal level. Measure the current input to the motor at this pressure

  7. Test operations.

  8. Measure the minimum operating pressure.

  9. Measure the minimum operating voltage of the opening and closing magnet.

  10. Check the operating pressure of the safety valve.

 

 

Pressure Switches & Gauges

  1. Test the density and pressure switches:
            - Switch B4 (SF6 : General lockout, loss of SF6)
            - Switch B3 (oil pressure : Auto-re-closure lockout, Motor control)
            - Switch B2 (oil pressure : General lockout, minimum pressure lockout ON)
            - Switch B1 (loss of nitrogen)

  2. Connect up a test gauge and test the SF6 pressure gauge

  3. Connect up a test gauge and test the hydraulic pressure gauge

  4. Check the current consumption of the anti-condensation heaters

  5. Check the enforced triple-pole operation feature and the anti-pumping feature

  6. Check all fully assembled breakers for leaks

  7. Tighten all terminals.
