Introduction

This document lists Equipment Inspection Checklist I2, which must be performed for Becker compressor type SV 250 max pressure/150 discharge every 6 months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses,... etc
  • Switch off 380 volt AC supply to compressor.
  • Switch off 220 volt DC control power
  • Use standard personal safety equipment, safety helmet, safety glasses.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard Personal safety equipment, Safety Helmet, safety glasses

Work to be Carried Out 

Note: The valves of all compressor stages must be inspected and cleaned from all contamination and only carbon deposits every 6 months.

It is good practice to have a spare set of compressor valves.

  1. Remove air filter and take out the round Hitler cartridge, use compressed air to blow out the filter element from the inside to the outside.

  2. Remove first stage cylinder head, inspect and clean/replace suction valve and outlet supply valve. Take care  that the valve are properly seated and the valve retainers are correctly placed and tightened.

  3. Remove second stage cylinder head, inspect and clean/ replace suction valve and outlet supply valve.

  4. Remove third stage cylinder, inspected clean/replace suction valve and outlet supply valve.

  5. Remove unloading valve block, clean and inspect.

  6. Properly clean out oil and water traps below the un looders.

  7. Switch on compressor to blow out supply lines from carbon deposits for each successive stage.

  8. In the same way blow out the oil cooling lines from oil/ water deposits.

  9. Check for oil tank, blades oil pump, and all pipe coupling connects.

  10. When all suction and supply are connected, test run the compressor and observing the correct gauge pressure for each stage.

  11. Check the oil pressure monitor switch; monetarily close the ball cock the compressor must shut down after about 10 seconds.

  12. Check the oil pressure gauge and check for the correct oil level in the reservoir but only when the compressor it at rest.

  13. Check the non-return valves from the main tank to be the compressor unit.

  14. Check the compressed air main tank, moment arily opening the botton drain valve to expel any water.
