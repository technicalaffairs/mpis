Introduction

Spare parts

The spare parts that are always or most frequently needed during on extraordinary maintenance have been marked with an asterisk
So, to minimize the breaker down-time, It is advisable to keet these parts handy in the maintenance workshop when an extraordinary maintenance is started
Besides the expendable parts, the recommended spare parts also include the gas seals which, even if found in apparently good conditions, must be replaced to ensure the required gas tightens to all joints of the SF6 enclosures
An additional time-saving precaution, consists in using three puffer-cylinder movable contact assemblies plus the relevant current-transfer contacts (i.e. items 47,48,54,55 and 56, fig 7 with the relevant accessories 47.1,.2,.3,.48.1,.2 and 56.1,.2,.3,.4) The operations from 1 (to 5) of paragraph
11.7.5 Can be therefore avoided or performed at a more convenient date, without extending the breaker down-time
To be sure of obtaining the correct spares, do not send orders for spare parts without indicating the item and figure numbers of the parts required and the serial number of the breaker for which the parts are intended
No spare parts are available for valves 92,93 and 99: these valves are available only as complete, factory assembled and tested units

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary steps for an extraordinary maintenance

  1. De-energize and connect to earth the high-voltage conductors to which the circuit breaker is connected

  2. Operate the breaker to the open position and then beenergize the breaker control circuit

  3. Open the condensate drain tap of the air receiver (see compressor instructions) and vent the compressed air from the piping system

  4. Disconnect all cables and pipes that terminate at the breaker poles

  5. Attach the SF6 handling and reconditfoning trailer to the gas-filling couplings of the breaker poles and recover as much gas as possible.

  6. Remove the bolts that joint the breaker poies to the adjacent equipment (fig. 6)

  7. Sting the breaker as shown in figure 4, take the weight of the breaker on the crane, remove the anchor bolts              (132.5 fig. 5), detach the breaker from the adjacent equipment, and finally bring it to a clean workshop, provided with a 3 ton hoist having at least a 6.5 m hook's helghl

Fitting and centering the breaker terminals and grading shields (fig. 13)

  1. Using  an allen key, install the breaker terminals (24-51, fig. 13), leaving them free to move within their fixing holes. Do not tighten the screws (22, fig. 9-49, fig. 7)

  2. Secure the template (135, fig. 13) to the breaker coupling tianges (6): this will automatically bring the terminals to the flange center line.
    Using a box wrench, slacken the screws (22, fig. 9-49, fig.7) completely and displace the studs till they are periectly centered (the fixing holes have a larger diameter)

  3. Tighten the terminal fixing screws.

  4. Remove the template and apply the shields (23, fig. 9-50, fig. 7)

  5. Lining up the breaker terminals and the fixed contacts
