Introduction

This procedures to be performed on an off load relay test. Look at the EEC panel name plate a check that this EEC system designation. 

From as built drawing determine the terminal number for the current circuits X1-1 X1-2C X1- 31X1-4  Eo, IA, Ib, Ic, Io, respectively from this wiring use a clip on ammeter to sure there is no warrant flowing through clip on ammeter relaying LZ96 zero current this sure that the line breakers are switched off.

From as built drawing determine the no of terminal voltage X1-Sc X1-61 X1-7 anj X1-8 for Va, Vb, Vc, and Vo respectively use multimeter for measuring AC voltage it will beading zero voltage this sure that the line breakers are switched off.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued, the maintenance staff should be qualified & carry suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, safety shoes … etc.

  • Line isolation and work permit required .

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current .

  • Switch off DC power supply before in setting or with drawing with drawing any card from the relay.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test set XS 924, ABB

  • Test plug XX91, Test socket XX91

  • Multimeter, Clip on ammeter

Work to be Carried Out

Steps to prepare for relay testing

  1. Review technical reference material Mana Facture’s manual

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from Field maintenance file.

  3. Abstain outage for line protected by this relay .

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged .

  5.  Inspect  the C.T secondary circuits the V.T Maintainer circuit breakers (MCB, S )and all cabling supplying the relay 

  6. Before inserting the test plug handle into the relay check from the station drawing that the  test plug will isolate 

               A1 - A2 Open-Disable tip , coil 1 of breaker 1

               A3 - A4 Open-Disable other outputs .

               A5 - A6 Open-Disable tip , coil 2 of  breaker 1

               A7 - A8 Open-Disable tip , coil 1 of  breaker 2

               A9 - A10 Open-Disable tip , coil 2 of  breaker 2

               A11 and A12 are connected to a trip output “D” used to stop timer.
                    *  Breaker trip coils and transfer trip scheme,
                    * Breaker Failure scheme associated with this relay,
                    * Auto reclosure associated with this relay,
                    * Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with the relay.

              If  the test plug does not isolate any one of above items , you have to isolate it .
